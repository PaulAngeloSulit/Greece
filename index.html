Introduction to Greece

Greece, often called the cradle of Western civilization, is a country rich in history, philosophy, art, and culture. Known for its stunning islands, ancient ruins, and Mediterranean charm, Greece offers a unique blend of natural beauty and historical legacy that continues to inspire the world today.

The Acropolis

The Acropolis is an ancient citadel located on a rocky, high ground in Athens, Greece, and is one of the most famous ancient sites in the world

Santorini

Santorini is a Greek volcanic island in the Aegean Sea, famous for its stunning caldera views, cliffside villages with whitewashed buildings and blue-domed churches, and breathtaking sunsets.

Meteora

a stunning UNESCO World Heritage site in central Greece known for its unique rock formations and historic Eastern Orthodox monasteries perched atop towering sandstone pillars

Greek Culture and Traditions

Greek culture is rich and lively, shaped by ancient history and strong family values. People enjoy music, dancing, and festivals throughout the year. Hospitality is very important, and sharing food with others is a common way to show kindness and friendship.

athens epidaurus festival

a major annual arts festival in Greece, running from June to August, that features world-class theater, dance, music, and opera

Bouzouki

The bouzouki holds a central place in Greek tradition, most notably as the defining instrument of rebetiko music, a genre often called the "Greek blues"

Moussaka

a layered casserole, most famously Greek, featuring roasted eggplant and/or potatoes, a rich meat sauce, and a creamy béchamel topping

Greek Arts

Greek art refers to the visual and applied arts, including architecture, created by the ancient Greeks, focusing on naturalistic and idealized depictions of gods, heroes, and humans, and emphasizing arete (excellence) through proportion, harmony, and realism

Greek History

Greek history spans from prehistoric times to the modern era, beginning with Stone Age hunter-gatherers and the Bronze Age civilizations of the Minoans and Mycenaeans

Minoans

a Bronze Age Aegean civilization that flourished on the island of Crete from approximately 3000 to 1100 BCE

Mycenaeans

a warlike, Bronze Age civilization in Greece from approximately 1600 to 1100 BCE that dominated the Aegean region
